Homemade Smash Burger


Try this homemade burger

Feel like having a super delicious burger? Yes?! Follow along to prepare one for yourself❣

Ingredients

  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened, or as needed
  • 1 pound ground chuck beef (80% lean)
  • 4 to 6-inch squares parchment paper
  • salt
  • 4 slices of American cheese
  • burger toppings of your choice

Instructions

  1. Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat and lightly oil the grate. Set a cast iron flat-top griddle or large cast iron skillet onto the grill and preheat until smoking.
  2. Spread butter on the insides of the buns. Toast the flat top until it turns a bit brown.
  3. Form meat into 8 loosely-packed balls, 2 ounces each.
    Do not pack the meat tightly, as this will prevent it from smashing properly.
    Place each ball on the hot flat-top, cover with a piece of parchment
    and immediately smash down to a 1/4 inch thickness using 2 stiff, sturdy spatulas
    that are criss-crossed to get proper leverage as you press down.
    Sprinkle the meat with salt.
  4. Grill for about 45 seconds, until the edges are dark brown and the centers are a light pink color.
    Using a bench scraper or firm spatula, gently scrape up the patties, flip over and immediately cover 4 of them with cheese.
    Grill an additional 15 to 20 seconds. Stack the plain patties over the cheese-covered patties so you have 4 stacks.
  5. Move each stack to a bun and serve with your favorite toppings.
    If you only have one spatula, wrap your other hand in an old dish towel for protection,
    and use it to press down on the spatula with both hands.
